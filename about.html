About

What is AERSI?

AERSI — the Air Exposure Severity Index — is an independent research project building a better way to understand air pollution in India.

Standard AQI answers one question: how bad is the air right now? AERSI answers three: how intense, how persistent, and how volatile is the exposure — over time, not just in this moment.

The problem

Why AQI isn't enough

📸 Snapshots miss the pattern

AQI captures a moment. A city that has moderate air on the day you check might have had extreme pollution for the previous three weeks. AERSI captures that accumulated story.

📅 Persistence compounds risk

Breathing mildly bad air every day is fundamentally different from breathing very bad air once a week. Long-term exposure to persistent pollution is the primary driver of chronic health damage.

📈 Volatility causes acute harm

When air quality swings unpredictably from clean to toxic, people cannot adapt. Sudden spikes trigger acute cardiovascular and respiratory events — especially for vulnerable populations.

⚖️ All pollutants aren't equal

Standard AQI reports the worst single pollutant. AERSI combines all major pollutants with weights based on their actual health burden — giving PM2.5 the weight it deserves.

How it runs

The Data Pipeline

Fully automated. Runs every day at 10:30 AM IST without any manual intervention.

🌐

Fetch from CPCB API

Daily snapshot pulled from the Central Pollution Control Board via data.gov.in. Covers all 547 active monitoring stations across India.

📦

Build 30-day rolling dataset

New snapshot is merged with the previous 29 days. Missing days are detected and flagged. The dataset grows until the 30-day window is full.

🧮

Compute AQI and AERSI

CPCB sub-index AQI is computed per station per day. Then PL, EPF, and VSF are calculated and multiplied to produce each station's AERSI score.

🗺️

Generate map and outputs

Interactive Folium map is rebuilt with fresh scores. All outputs are committed back to the GitHub repository automatically.

🌍

Site updates live

The website reads directly from the committed CSV files. No backend required — the data is always the latest committed version.
