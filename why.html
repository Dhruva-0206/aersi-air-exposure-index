Why AERSI?

AQI is a snapshot.
Exposure is a story.

Standard air quality indices were designed to answer one question: how bad is the air at this moment? But chronic health damage from air pollution is driven by something far more complex — and AERSI is built to capture it.

"The burden of air pollution on human health is not determined by the worst single day — it is determined by the cumulative weight of every day, every spike, every season of exposure."

The Problem

What AQI misses

Standard AQI answers:

  • ✓ How bad is the air right now?
  • ✗ How often has it been unsafe?
  • ✗ Is this pollution stable or swinging?
  • ✗ What is the accumulated exposure over time?
  • ✗ Which pollutants are driving the danger?
  • ✗ How does today compare to last month?

AERSI answers:

  • ✓ How polluted is the air today, weighted by health impact?
  • ✓ How many of the last 30 days exceeded safe thresholds?
  • ✓ How unpredictably is the air quality swinging?
  • ✓ What is the composite severity of exposure over time?
  • ✓ Which stations are the worst for long-term health?
  • ✓ Is a station getting better or worse as data grows?

The Multi-Pollutant Problem
